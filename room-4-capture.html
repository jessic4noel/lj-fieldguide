IV

The Capture

posted by: jess_ru

mood: hollowed  ·  music: server fans, fluorescent hum

tags: ddos, bloggers law, sup, rambler, telegram

Artist statement. ЖЖ was emptied without being banned. This room reconstructs the capture as administrative weather rather than a single takedown — DDoS waves on opposition blogs, the 2007 sale to SUP, absorption into the Rambler ecosystem, the 2014 bloggers' law, the relocation of servers to Russia, and the slow user exodus to Facebook and then Telegram. The question the room asks: what kind of capture is this, exactly?

Room in progress

This room will follow the same scrollytelling pattern demonstrated in Room I:

Step 1 · DDoS timeline. Opposition-blog outages, 2010–2012; what the attacks targeted and when.
Step 2 · The SUP sale (2007) and the Rambler absorption. Ownership-transfer documents; what changed in moderation and infrastructure.
Step 3 · The 2014 bloggers' law. Excerpted statute; the 3,000-readers threshold; servers relocated to Russia.
Step 4 · The exodus. Facebook → Telegram; what survived in diaspora; what didn't.
Step 5 · The platform today. Screenshots of livejournal.com 2026; the empty room.

Sources: Nossik (2014), Soldatov & Borogan (2015), and Sanovich, Stukal, & Tucker (2018). The full build will mirror Room I's sticky-panel layout.
