The Living Room

posted by: jess_ru

mood: prolific  ·  music: kitchen kettle, distant

tags: poetry, humor, leibov, gorny

Artist statement. A literary platform — possibly the only true one the Russian internet ever had. This room's argument is that LJ's architecture made a specifically writerly public possible: long-form posts gave room to think, threaded comments turned readers into co-authors, and the chronological feed rewarded daily practice over virality. Two genres organize the room: occasional political poetry (after Leibov) and threaded humor (after Gorny). The wartime poetry continuum runs through to 2022–.

Room in progress

This room will follow the same scrollytelling pattern demonstrated in Room I:

Step 1 · Leibov on occasional political poetry. Excerpt from the Gorham/Lunde/Paulsen 2014 chapter, paired with a sample comment-thread poem.
Step 2 · Gorny on humor and jokes. The threaded joke as a social form; LJ's comment tree as a venue for collaborative wit.
Step 3 · A wartime poem in a thread. 2022– continuation of the genre — circulation through reposts, comments, friends-only locks.
Step 4 · Why this couldn't happen on Twitter. Length, threading, durability, friend-graph trust.

The full build will mirror Room I's left/right scrollytelling layout (sticky artifact panel + scrolling captions). For now this stub establishes the navigation skeleton.
