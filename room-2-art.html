толерантность не катит

posted by: zoltan_partosh Kirill Medvedev

Ребёнок спрашивает, папа, бог есть? Я говорю ему, это кому как кажется. Кто-то верит, а кто-то нет. Он говорит, не, ну понятно, а вообще-то он есть?

По-моему, безвыходная, на сегодняшний день, ситуация.

A child asks, papa, is there a god? I tell him, it depends on each person Some believe, some don't. He says, no, well, I see, but does He actually exist?

In my opinion, a hopeless situation, as of today.

On 20 May 2008, at 11:19 PM, Medvedev posted a five-sentence poem titled "толерантность не катит" (tolerance doesn't fly) to his LiveJournal account. The brief, yet haunting poem, is reflective of much of the political poetry published by LJ’s artistic community. In this poem, a child asks his father whether God exists and is unimpressed by his father's relativist answer. The post closes with a single sentence of judgment: "In my opinion, a hopeless situation, for today." Medvedev hints that the oppression of the Russian regime creates such a hopeless life, God’s presence is inconsequential. Medvedev, like most other artists, was drawn to LiveJournal as an act of political protest. In the early twenty-first century, Medvedev declared that, in an act of protest, he would not release any of his artistic work with any copyright institutions who were actively allocating Russia's cultural capital. He moved his platform to LiveJournal, which was safe from financial ties to the Russian regime, and devoted the publication of his political essays, poems, and activism to the site.
